2. Reasoning Questions

Q6. Transition Metals & Color

Due to presence of unpaired d-electrons. They absorb energy from visible light to undergo d-d transitions. Complementary color is observed[cite: 265, 266].

Q7. Transition Metals as Catalysts

  • Show variable oxidation states (form unstable intermediates)[cite: 269].
  • Provide large surface area for reactants[cite: 270].

Q8. Lanthanide Contraction

Def: Steady decrease in atomic/ionic radii of Lanthanides with increasing atomic number[cite: 272].

Cause: Poor shielding effect of 4f electrons[cite: 273].

Q9. Phenol vs Alcohol Acidity

Phenoxide ion is stabilized by resonance. Alkoxide ion is unstable due to electron-donating (+I) effect of alkyl group[cite: 275, 276].

Q10. Haloarenes Reactivity

Less reactive towards nucleophilic substitution because Resonance gives C-Cl bond a partial double bond character (shorter and stronger)[cite: 278, 279].

3. Definitions & Short Notes

Q11. Ligand & Coordination No.

Ligand: Ion/molecule donating electron pair to central metal (e.g., NH₃, CN⁻)[cite: 286].

Coordination No: Total coordinate bonds formed with central metal[cite: 288].

Q12. Denaturation of Protein

When subjected to heat/pH change, H-bonds break, globules unfold, helix uncoils. Protein loses biological activity (e.g., boiling egg)[cite: 290, 291].

Q13. SHE (Standard Hydrogen Electrode)

Reference electrode with 0.00 V potential[cite: 293].

Construction: Pt wire with Pt black, 1M H⁺ solution, H₂ gas at 1 bar[cite: 294].

Q14. Vitamins

  • Vit A: Night Blindness[cite: 296].
  • Vit C: Scurvy (Water soluble)[cite: 297].
  • Vit D: Rickets[cite: 298].

Q15. Osmotic Pressure

Minimum excess pressure on solution side to prevent solvent flow through semi-permeable membrane[cite: 300].

π = CRT [cite: 301]
