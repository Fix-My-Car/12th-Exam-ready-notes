Q6. State Lenz's Law

"The polarity of induced EMF is such that it tends to produce a current which opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it."

Significance: It is based on the Law of Conservation of Energy.

Q7. What is Total Internal Reflection (TIR)? Write conditions.

Definition: When light travels from a denser to a rarer medium and the angle of incidence is greater than the Critical Angle, the light reflects back into the denser medium.

Condition 1: Light must travel from Denser to Rarer medium

Condition 2: Angle of incidence (i) > Critical angle (c)

Q8. Properties of Electric Field Lines

  1. They start from Positive charge and end at Negative charge
  2. Two field lines never intersect each other
  3. They are always perpendicular to the surface of a conductor

Q9. Properties of Electromagnetic Waves

  1. They travel with the speed of light (3 × 10⁸ m/s) in vacuum
  2. They are transverse in nature
  3. They do not require a material medium to propagate

Q10. Why does the sky appear blue?

Due to Scattering of light. Air molecules scatter shorter wavelengths (Blue/Violet) more than longer wavelengths (Red). Our eyes are more sensitive to Blue.

Q11. Why is the core of a transformer laminated?

To reduce energy loss due to Eddy Currents. Laminations break the path of these circulating currents.

Q6. Why do Transition Metals form colored ions?

Due to the presence of unpaired d-electrons. These electrons absorb energy from visible light to undergo d-d transitions (jumping from lower to higher energy d-orbitals). The complementary color is observed.

Q7. Why are Transition Metals good catalysts?

  1. They show variable oxidation states, allowing them to form unstable intermediates
  2. They provide a large surface area for reactants to adsorb

Q8. What is Lanthanide Contraction? Write its cause.

Definition: The steady decrease in the atomic and ionic radii of Lanthanide elements with increasing atomic number.

Cause: The poor shielding effect of 4f electrons. They cannot effectively shield the outer electrons from the increasing nuclear charge.

Q9. Why is Phenol more acidic than Alcohol?

The Phenoxide ion (formed after Phenol loses H⁺) is stabilized by resonance (delocalization of negative charge). The Alkoxide ion (from Alcohol) is unstable due to the electron-donating (+I) effect of the alkyl group.

Q10. Why are Haloarenes less reactive towards nucleophilic substitution?

Due to Resonance, the lone pair on Chlorine interacts with the benzene ring, giving the C-Cl bond a partial double bond character. This makes the bond shorter and stronger, making it difficult to break.

Q11. Define Ligand and Coordination Number

Ligand: An ion or molecule that donates a pair of electrons to the central metal atom to form a coordinate bond (e.g., NH₃, CN⁻)

Coordination Number: The total number of coordinate bonds formed between the central metal and ligands

Q12. What is Denaturation of Protein?

When a protein is subjected to heat or pH change, hydrogen bonds break, globules unfold, and the helix uncoils. The protein loses its biological activity (e.g., boiling of egg, curdling of milk).

Q13. Explain Standard Hydrogen Electrode (SHE)

It is a reference electrode with potential arbitrarily fixed at 0.00V. Construction: A platinum wire coated with Pt black is dipped in 1M H⁺ solution, and pure H₂ gas is bubbled at 1bar pressure.

Q14. Vitamins A, C, and D

  • Vitamin A: Deficiency causes Night Blindness
  • Vitamin C: Deficiency causes Scurvy. Water-soluble, cannot be stored
  • Vitamin D: Deficiency causes Rickets (bone deformities)

The minimum excess pressure that must be applied to the solution side to prevent the flow of solvent into it through a semi-permeable membrane.

Chapter 1: The Last Lesson

2 Marks

Q1. What was Franz expected to be prepared with?

Franz was expected to be prepared with a lesson on Participles. His teacher, M. Hamel, had announced that he would question students on this topic, but Franz did not know even the first word about it.

Q2. What did Franz notice that was unusual?

Usually there was great bustle, but that day everything was as quiet as Sunday morning. The most surprising thing was seeing village elders sitting quietly on the back benches that were usually empty.

Q3. How did Franz's feelings change? (3 Marks)

When Franz learned it was the last French lesson, he felt sudden love for his language and regretted neglecting studies. His books, which seemed a burden before, now felt like old friends. He forgot M. Hamel's "cranky" nature because he felt sorry the teacher was leaving after 40 years.

Chapter 2: Lost Spring

2-3 Marks

Q4. What is Saheb looking for? Where from?

Saheb is looking for "gold" (coins, notes, anything valuable) in garbage. He came from Dhaka, Bangladesh, after storms swept away his family's fields and homes.

Q5. What makes Firozabad famous?

Firozabad is the center of India's glass-blowing industry. It is famous for its bangles. Every other family is engaged in making bangles for women in India.

Q6. Hazards of working in glass bangles industry (3 Marks)

  • Workers, including children, work in high temperatures in dingy cells without air or light
  • Lose eyesight at young age due to dust from polishing glass
  • Work in dark hutments next to flickering oil lamps

Chapter 3: Deep Water

2-3 Marks

Q7. What is the "misadventure"?

At the YMCA pool, a "big bruiser" of a boy tossed eighteen-year-old Douglas into the deep end. Douglas almost drowned and developed deep fear of water.

Q8. How did instructor "build a swimmer"? (3 Marks)

The instructor worked step-by-step: First used rope and pulley for safety. Then taught Douglas to exhale underwater and inhale above it. Finally taught him to kick legs. After months of integrated practice, Douglas became confident swimmer.

Chapter 4: The Rattrap

2-3 Marks

Q9. Where did peddler get idea of world being rattrap?

While plodding along road, peddler thought about his own rattraps. Realized world offers riches and joys as baits, just like rattrap offers cheese. Once tempted, world closes in on you.

Q10. Why sign as Captain von Stahle? (3 Marks)

Edla treated peddler with kindness and respect, as if he were real Captain, even after knowing he was thief. This "noble treatment" awakened his goodness. He signed as Captain to show he had changed and wanted to return stolen money.

My Mother at Sixty-Six

Q11. Why mother compared to 'late winter's moon'? (2 Marks)

The late winter's moon looks pale, hazy, and lacks brightness. Similarly, the poet's mother, due to old age, looks pale and has lost her youthful glow and vitality.

Q12. What do parting words signify? (2 Marks)

Her words, "See you soon, Amma," signify hope to see mother alive again. It is brave attempt to hide childhood fear of losing mother behind a cheerful facade.

Keeping Quiet

Q13. What is the "sadness"? (3 Marks)

The sadness arises from never understanding ourselves and constantly threatening ourselves with death. We are so busy with "single-minded" progress that we forget to enjoy the silence that could heal this sadness.

A Thing of Beauty

Q14. List things of beauty mentioned (2 Marks)

The Sun, the Moon, old and young trees, simple sheep, daffodils, clear rills (streams), and the "mighty dead" stories are all listed as things of beauty.

The Tiger King

Q15. Who is Tiger King? Why that name? (2 Marks)

The Tiger King is the Maharaja of Pratibandapuram. He got this name because an astrologer predicted his death would come from a tiger, so he vowed to kill a hundred tigers to disprove the prophecy.

Q16. How did hundredth tiger take revenge? (3 Marks)

The King bought a wooden toy tiger for his son. A tiny sliver of wood from the toy pierced the King's hand, causing severe infection. Despite surgery by three doctors, the King died. Thus, the hundredth tiger (the toy) took revenge.

The Enemy

Q17. Who was Dr. Sadao? Where was his house? (2 Marks)

Dr. Sadao Hoki was a famous Japanese surgeon and scientist. His house was built on a square stone foundation on the Japanese coast, surrounded by pine trees.

Q18. How did Dr. Sadao help American soldier escape? (3 Marks)

Dr. Sadao provided the soldier with his boat, food, and extra clothing. Instructed him to row to nearby island and wait for Korean fishing boat. Also gave him flashlight to signal in case of danger.

Grammar & Writing

Target: 10/10 Marks in Section C + Full Marks in Writing

✍️ EXAM CHEAT SHEET

Before you write the answer, check these 3 things:

1. -ING?

Did I write being?

2. DID NOT?

Did I change verb to V1? (did not play, did not do)

3. UNLESS?

Did I remove the not from the first part?

1 Passive Voice (Change the Voice)

Object + Helping Verb + V3 + by + Subject

Tense Active Passive Rule Passive
Simple Past He wrote a letter was/were + V3 A letter was written by him
Continuous He is playing cricket being + V3 Cricket is being played by him
Perfect He has done the work been + V3 The work has been done by him
Modal I can lift this box be + V3 This box can be lifted by me

CAUTION: If the sentence has -ing (playing, singing), you MUST write being in the answer.

2 If and Unless

Unless = If + not

Type A: Replacing 'If...not' with 'Unless'

Question:

If you do not work hard, you will fail.

Answer:

Unless you work hard, you will fail.

Rule: Remove 'If' and 'not'. Rest remains same.

Type B: Replacing 'If' (positive) with 'Unless'

Question:

If it rains, crops will grow.

Answer:

Unless it rains, crops will not grow.

Rule: Add 'not' to second part to keep meaning true.

3 Too...to / So...that

Remove too → use so. Remove to → use that + Subject + cannot

Question:

He is too weak to walk.

Answer:

He is so weak that he cannot walk.

Note: If past tense ("He was too weak..."), use could not

4 No sooner...than

No sooner + did + Subject + V1... + than

Question:

As soon as the thief saw the police, he ran away.

Answer:

No sooner did the thief see the police than he ran away.

⚠️ TRAPS:

  1. Always change verb to V1 (saw → see)
  2. Always use than, not then

5 Negative Sentences (The "Did" Trap)

When main verb is do/does/did, you must add DO

Question:

He did his work.

Correct:

He did not do his work. ✅

Wrong: He did not his work. ❌

Present Tense:

She shuts the door.

→ She does not shut the door.

Past Tense:

She shut the door.

→ She did not shut the door.

6 Relative Clauses (Who/Which)

Use 'Who' for people. Replace pronoun (He/She) with Relative Pronoun.

The boy is my brother.

He is wearing a red cap.

The boy who is wearing a red cap is my brother.

1. Articles (A, An, The)

An:

Used before vowel sounds (A, E, I, O, U sound)

  • • He is an M.L.A. (Sound is 'Em' - vowel)
  • • He is an honest man. (Sound is 'Onest' - H is silent)

A:

Used before consonant sounds

  • • He is a University student. (Sound is 'Yoo' - consonant)

The:

Used for unique things or superlatives

  • • The Sun, The Ganga, The highest mountain

2. Prepositions

Between vs Among

• Distribute sweets between Ram and Shyam. (Two people)

• Distribute sweets among the children. (More than two)

Since vs For

• I have been playing since morning. (Point of time: Morning, 1998, Monday)

• I have been playing for two hours. (Duration)

In vs Into

• The boy is in the room. (Rest position)

• The boy jumped into the river. (Motion)

3. Determiners (Much/Many/Any/Some)

Much

Uncountable nouns

There isn't much milk in the glass.

Many

Countable nouns

I have many friends.

Some

Positive sentences

I have some money.

Any

Negative or Questions

I do not have any money.

Do you have any money?

4. NOTE MAKING & SUMMARY (4 Marks)

How to Find a Suitable Title (1 Mark):

  • • The "Repeated Word" Strategy: Scan passage. If word appears many times (e.g., "Discipline", "Trees", "Pollution"), use it in title.
  • • Format: Capitalize first letter of main words
  • • Examples: Importance of Games and Sports, Benefits of Early Rising, Pollution: A Great Threat
