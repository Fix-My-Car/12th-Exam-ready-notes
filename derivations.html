Step-by-Step Proofs (Q16 - Q25)

Q16

Gauss's Theorem Proof

Statement: The total electric flux through a closed surface is equal to $\frac{1}{\epsilon_0}$ times the net charge enclosed.

Consider a sphere of radius $r$ enclosing a point charge $q$ at its center.

Step 1: Definition of Flux

Small electric flux $d\phi$ through area element $d\vec{S}$ is:

$$ d\phi = \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{S} = E \, dS \cos\theta $$

Step 2: Apply Symmetry

For a sphere, $\vec{E}$ is radial and $d\vec{S}$ is also radial (normal to surface). Thus, angle $\theta = 0^\circ$.

$$ d\phi = E \, dS \cos 0^\circ = E \, dS $$

Total flux $\phi = \oint E \, dS$

Step 3: Substitute Electric Field

We know field of a point charge is $E = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{q}{r^2}$. Since $E$ is constant on surface:

$$ \phi = E \oint dS = \left( \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{q}{r^2} \right) \times (\text{Total Area}) $$

Step 4: Final Result

Surface area of sphere $\oint dS = 4\pi r^2$.

$$ \phi = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{q}{r^2} \times 4\pi r^2 $$ $$ \phi = \frac{q}{\epsilon_0} $$
Q17

Electric Field of Dipole (Axial Position)

Consider a dipole with charges $-q$ (at A) and $+q$ (at B) separated by $2a$. Point P is at distance $r$ from the center O on the axis.

Step 1: Individual Fields

Field due to $+q$ (at distance $r-a$): $E_B = \frac{kq}{(r-a)^2}$ (Away)

Field due to $-q$ (at distance $r+a$): $E_A = \frac{kq}{(r+a)^2}$ (Towards)

Step 2: Net Field

Since $E_B > E_A$, Net field $E = E_B - E_A$.

$$ E = kq \left[ \frac{1}{(r-a)^2} - \frac{1}{(r+a)^2} \right] $$

Step 3: Simplify

$$ E = kq \left[ \frac{(r+a)^2 - (r-a)^2}{(r^2-a^2)^2} \right] $$

Using $(A+B)^2 - (A-B)^2 = 4AB$:

$$ E = kq \left[ \frac{4ar}{(r^2-a^2)^2} \right] = \frac{k (q \cdot 2a) \cdot 2r}{(r^2-a^2)^2} $$

Step 4: Final Approximation

Dipole moment $p = q \cdot 2a$. For short dipole ($r \gg a$), neglect $a^2$.

$$ E = \frac{k \cdot p \cdot 2r}{r^4} = \frac{2kp}{r^3} $$ $$ E = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{2p}{r^3} $$
Q18

Capacitance of Parallel Plate Capacitor

Two parallel plates of area $A$ separated by distance $d$. Charge density is $\sigma = Q/A$.

Step 1: Electric Field

Electric field between two plates is uniform:

$$ E = \frac{\sigma}{\epsilon_0} = \frac{Q}{A\epsilon_0} $$

Step 2: Potential Difference

Relation between $V$ and $E$ is $V = E \cdot d$.

$$ V = \left( \frac{Q}{A\epsilon_0} \right) \cdot d $$

Step 3: Capacitance Formula

By definition $C = Q/V$.

$$ C = \frac{Q}{\left( \frac{Qd}{A\epsilon_0} \right)} $$ $$ C = \frac{\epsilon_0 A}{d} $$
Q19

Wheatstone Bridge Principle

Four resistors P, Q, R, S form a bridge. In balanced condition, Galvanometer current $I_g = 0$.

Step 1: Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (Loop 1)

Apply KVL to loop ABDA (Top triangle):

$$ -I_1 P - I_g G + I_2 R = 0 $$

Since balanced ($I_g = 0$):

$$ I_1 P = I_2 R \quad \text{...(i)} $$

Step 2: Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (Loop 2)

Apply KVL to loop BCDB (Bottom triangle):

$$ -I_1 Q + I_2 S + I_g G = 0 $$

Since balanced ($I_g = 0$):

$$ I_1 Q = I_2 S \quad \text{...(ii)} $$

Step 3: Divide Equations

Divide equation (i) by (ii):

$$ \frac{I_1 P}{I_1 Q} = \frac{I_2 R}{I_2 S} $$ $$ \frac{P}{Q} = \frac{R}{S} $$
Q20

Biot-Savart Law (Center of Coil)

Magnetic field at the center of a current carrying circular loop of radius $R$.

Step 1: Biot-Savart Law Formula

$$ dB = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I dl \sin\theta}{r^2} $$

Step 2: Apply Conditions

For a circle, the element $dl$ is always perpendicular to radius $R$. So $\theta = 90^\circ$ and $r=R$.

$$ dB = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I dl \sin 90^\circ}{R^2} = \frac{\mu_0 I}{4\pi R^2} dl $$

Step 3: Integration

Total field $B = \int dB$. The only variable is $dl$.

$$ B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{4\pi R^2} \int dl $$

Total length of loop $\int dl = 2\pi R$ (Circumference).

Step 4: Final Result

$$ B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{4\pi R^2} (2\pi R) $$ $$ B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2R} $$
Q21

Ampere's Circuital Law (Solenoid)

Long solenoid with $n$ turns per unit length. Consider a rectangular Amperian loop PQRS partly inside and partly outside.

Step 1: Line Integral of B

$\oint \vec{B}\cdot d\vec{l}$ over PQRS is sum of 4 parts:

  • PQ (Inside): $B \cdot L$ (B is parallel to length)
  • QR & SP (Perpendicular): 0 (B is perpendicular to path)
  • RS (Outside): 0 (Field outside ideal solenoid is 0)
$$ \oint \vec{B}\cdot d\vec{l} = BL $$

Step 2: Current Enclosed

Number of turns in length $L$ is $N = nL$.

Total current enclosed $I_{enc} = N \cdot I = nLI$.

Step 3: Apply Ampere's Law

$$ \oint \vec{B}\cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 I_{enc} $$ $$ BL = \mu_0 (nLI) $$

Step 4: Final Result

Canceling $L$ from both sides:

$$ B = \mu_0 n I $$
Q22

LCR Circuit & Resonance

Series LCR circuit connected to source $V = V_0 \sin\omega t$.

Step 1: Phasor Relationships

  • $V_R$ is in phase with current $I$.
  • $V_L$ leads current by $90^\circ$.
  • $V_C$ lags current by $90^\circ$.

Step 2: Net Voltage (Pythagoras)

The net voltage $V$ is the vector sum:

$$ V^2 = V_R^2 + (V_L - V_C)^2 $$ $$ V = \sqrt{(IR)^2 + (IX_L - IX_C)^2} $$

Step 3: Impedance (Z)

Dividing by $I$ gives Impedance $Z = V/I$:

$$ Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2} $$

Step 4: Resonance Condition

Resonance occurs when current is max (Impedance is min). This happens when $X_L = X_C$.

$$ \omega L = \frac{1}{\omega C} \Rightarrow \omega^2 = \frac{1}{LC} $$ $$ 2\pi f = \frac{1}{\sqrt{LC}} \Rightarrow f = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}} $$
Q23

Lens Maker's Formula

Refraction through a lens with two spherical surfaces of radii $R_1$ and $R_2$.

Step 1: First Surface Refraction

Light travels from air ($\mu_1$) to lens ($\mu_2$). Image forms at $v'$.

$$ \frac{\mu_2}{v'} - \frac{\mu_1}{u} = \frac{\mu_2-\mu_1}{R_1} \quad \text{...(i)} $$

Step 2: Second Surface Refraction

Light travels from lens ($\mu_2$) to air ($\mu_1$). The virtual object is at $v'$, final image at $v$.

$$ \frac{\mu_1}{v} - \frac{\mu_2}{v'} = \frac{\mu_1-\mu_2}{R_2} $$

Rearranging the RHS ($\mu_1-\mu_2 = -(\mu_2-\mu_1)$):

$$ \frac{\mu_1}{v} - \frac{\mu_2}{v'} = - \frac{\mu_2-\mu_1}{R_2} \quad \text{...(ii)} $$

Step 3: Combine Equations

Add (i) and (ii):

$$ \frac{\mu_1}{v} - \frac{\mu_1}{u} = (\mu_2-\mu_1) \left( \frac{1}{R_1} - \frac{1}{R_2} \right) $$ $$ \frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{\mu_2-\mu_1}{\mu_1} \left( \frac{1}{R_1} - \frac{1}{R_2} \right) $$

Step 4: Lens Formula Substitution

We know $\frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}$ and let $\mu = \mu_2/\mu_1$.

$$ \frac{1}{f} = (\mu-1) \left( \frac{1}{R_1} - \frac{1}{R_2} \right) $$
Q24

Prism Formula

Step 1: Prism Geometry

For a prism with angle $A$, incident angle $i$, emergence angle $e$, and deviation $\delta$:

$$ A = r_1 + r_2 $$ $$ A + \delta = i + e $$

Step 2: Minimum Deviation Condition

At minimum deviation ($\delta = \delta_m$), the ray passes symmetrically.

So, $i = e$ and $r_1 = r_2 = r$.

Step 3: Substitute Values

From $A = r + r \Rightarrow A = 2r \Rightarrow r = A/2$.

From $A + \delta_m = i + i \Rightarrow 2i = A + \delta_m \Rightarrow i = \frac{A+\delta_m}{2}$.

Step 4: Snell's Law

Refractive index $\mu = \frac{\sin i}{\sin r}$. Substituting $i$ and $r$:

$$ \mu = \frac{\sin(\frac{A+\delta_m}{2})}{\sin(\frac{A}{2})} $$
Q25

Einstein's Photoelectric Equation

Based on Law of Conservation of Energy.

Step 1: Energy of Photon

Light consists of photons. Energy of one photon is:

$$ E = h\nu $$

Step 2: Utilization of Energy

When a photon strikes a metal surface, its energy is used in two parts:

  1. To overcome the attractive force of nucleus (Work Function $\phi_0$).
  2. To impart velocity to the electron (Kinetic Energy $K_{max}$).

Step 3: Equation Formulation

$$ \text{Energy of Photon} = \text{Work Function} + \text{Kinetic Energy} $$ $$ h\nu = \phi_0 + K_{max} $$

Step 4: Final Form

Rearranging for Kinetic Energy:

$$ K_{max} = h\nu - \phi_0 $$

Also written as: $\frac{1}{2}mv_{max}^2 = h(\nu - \nu_0)$
