2. Reasoning & Short Definitions (Enhanced)

Q6. State Lenz's Law

"The polarity of induced EMF is such that it tends to produce a current which opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it."

$\varepsilon = -\frac{d\Phi}{dt}$

(Negative sign indicates opposition)

Significance: It is based on the Law of Conservation of Energy.

Q7. Total Internal Reflection (TIR)

The phenomenon where a ray of light traveling from a denser to a rarer medium is completely reflected back into the denser medium if the angle of incidence exceeds the critical angle.

  • Condition 1: Light must travel from Denser $\to$ Rarer medium.
  • Condition 2: Angle of incidence > Critical angle ($i > i_c$).

Q8. Properties of Electric Field Lines

  • Start from Positive charge and end at Negative charge.
  • Two lines never intersect. (Reason: If they did, there would be two directions of E-field at one point, which is impossible).
  • They are always perpendicular to the surface of a conductor.
  • Tangent to the line gives direction of Electric Field.

Q9. Properties of EM Waves

  • Produced by accelerated charges.
  • Travel with speed of light ($c = 3 \times 10^8$ m/s) in vacuum.
  • Transverse in nature (E and B fields oscillate perpendicular to propagation).
  • Do not require a material medium.

Q10. Why is Sky Blue?

Due to Scattering of Light. According to Rayleigh's Law, scattering $\propto 1/\lambda^4$.

Air molecules scatter shorter wavelengths (Blue/Violet) much more than longer wavelengths (Red). Since our eyes are more sensitive to Blue than Violet, the sky appears blue.

Q11. Why Transformer Core is Laminated?

To reduce energy loss due to Eddy Currents.

Changing magnetic flux induces circulating currents (eddy currents) in the iron core, causing heat. Lamination (thin sheets insulated from each other) breaks the path of these currents, increasing resistance and minimizing heat loss.

3. Diagrams & Working (Full Descriptions)

Q12. P-N Junction as Full Wave Rectifier

Principle: Diode conducts only in forward bias.

Construction: Uses a center-tap transformer and two diodes ($D_1, D_2$).

Working:

  • Positive Half Cycle: $D_1$ is Forward Biased (ON), $D_2$ is Reverse Biased (OFF). Current flows via $D_1$.
  • Negative Half Cycle: $D_2$ is Forward Biased (ON), $D_1$ is Reverse Biased (OFF). Current flows via $D_2$.

Result: Unidirectional, continuous current through load.

Q13. Logic Gates

1. OR Gate ($Y = A + B$)

Output is High (1) if at least one input is High.

2. AND Gate ($Y = A \cdot B$)

Output is High (1) only if all inputs are High.

3. NOT Gate ($Y = \bar{A}$)

Output is the inverse of the input.

Q14. Compound Microscope

Construction: Consists of two convex lenses.

  • Objective Lens: Small focal length and small aperture (facing the object).
  • Eyepiece: Large focal length and large aperture (facing the eye).

Working: Objective forms a real, inverted, magnified image. Eyepiece acts as a simple magnifier to form the final virtual, inverted, and highly magnified image.

Q15. Forward vs Reverse Bias

  • Forward Bias: p-side connected to Positive, n-side to Negative. Depletion layer decreases. Current flows easily.
  • Reverse Bias: p-side connected to Negative, n-side to Positive. Depletion layer increases. No current flows (except negligible leakage).
